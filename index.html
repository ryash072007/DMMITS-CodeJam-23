Where is ISRO?
INCOSPAR (Indian National Committee for Space Research) was formed in 1962 to jump-start Indian research on space.
It employed people who specialized in aerodynamics and rocket fuel from Indian Ordnance Factories.
Under the umbrella of INCOSPAR, various experiments were conducted, including those related to cosmic radiation, high altitude, airborne testing, and other space phenomena.
However, to further speed up space research in India and achieve more significant milestones, ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) was created. It superseded INCOSPAR and took on a broader mandate.
ISRO, established under the newly formed Department of Space, continued the efforts initiated by INCOSPAR. It sent several satellites into space, utilizing Russian-made rockets, and conducted a wide range of experiments.

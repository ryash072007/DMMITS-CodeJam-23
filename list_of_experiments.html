SITE - Satellite Instructional Television Experiment

One of the first major experiments conducted was the SITE operation. ISRO wanted to test out sattelite capabilities in a time where it was still in its primitive form. In order to achieve this goal and to help the rural people of India, the SITE operation was launched in 1975. The SITE operation involved broadcasting information related to science, literature, and, finance to people in over 2000 villages. This operation is also credited with training 50,000 primary science teachers and helped over 200,000 people. Thus it was considered as the largest sociological experiment in the world at that time.

Mars Orbiter Mission - Mangalyaan

The Mars Orbiter Mission was the first interplanetary mission conducted by India and waas launched in november of 2013. This was the first Mars Mission to succeed in its first attempt in the world and the first asian country to do so. It was launched using a PSLV rocket and took 298 days to reach Mars. It is the least expensive Mars mission to date at around 450 crore rupees. This was only possible beacuse the team behind the mission decided to use a modular system for it.

INSAT - Indian National Satellite System

INSAT was commissioned in 1983 with the launch of INSAT-1B. It was a revolutionary system at that time and continues to be a pillar for broadcasting sector in India. It brought forward a television and audio revolution in India to where it is now. It is a thick network of 11 sattelites that are monitored and controlled by Master Control Facilities present in Bhopal and Hassan. The INSAT sattelites' provides threee diffrent electromagnetic bands for various purposes. The INSAT system also enables India to recieve SOS messages from the South Asian and Indian Ocean for search and rescue missions.
